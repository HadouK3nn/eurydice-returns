The Ache

You did not choose the underworld.

A serpent took Eurydice while she was walking through the grass — she did not step down into the dark, she was pulled. And so were you: by migration that unhoused you from the language of your own feeling, by a betrayal that broke something you thought was solid, by illness that ended the version of yourself you had been managing, by the long, uncounted labour of holding everyone else whole while no one held you.

You have been down there for a while now. Functioning. Holding the walls. And underneath the competence, a quiet knowledge that no one is coming to lead you back — no Orpheus, no rescue, no one whose love will turn and carry you up into the light.

What you long for, and rarely say: to return on your own terms. To come back not repaired, not smaller, not grateful to have survived — but fervent, fertile, and free.

The Story That Invites You In

You're here because something took you down without asking.

It arrived — as a border crossed, a marriage ended, a diagnosis, a long season of holding everyone else while no one held you. And you did what you have always done: you managed it. You held the walls. You grew, and you called it growth, and some of that was true.

And underneath the competence there is a knowledge you rarely say aloud: no one is coming to lead you back. There is no turn of someone else's love that carries you up into the light. You have been waiting, a little, without admitting it.

Eurydice did not choose the dark either. And the old story says she needed Orpheus to walk ahead and not look back, and he looked back, and she was lost. This is the other story. The one where no one's leadership can return her — where she has to lead herself. And she does.

The World Beyond the Threshold

We are in the Rhodope Mountains — a range that does not obey borders, that carries Bulgarian and Greek and Turkish and Pomak in one body of stone, the way you carry your own languages. My father's land. The air is resined and cold in the mornings. There is a cave the shape of the body that makes life, and we go into it not to hide but to conceive.

On the other side, a woman walks up out of the dark alone. No lyre leading her. No man ahead of her turning to check she is still there. Just her, ascending, in her own time.

And what she carries up is not a wound she has processed. It is something conceived in the dark and gestated slowly over nine weeks — a life, a work, a self she has not been yet — that she will deliver in her own place, in her own hour, held by the presence of women who descended alongside her and returned.

You can hear her own heart under the drums. You can taste salt — tears and laughter, the same water. She is not fixed. She is fervent.

Who This Is For — and Who I Am

I am someone who…

  • …cares about the people who live and love not where they were born — the intercultural, the multilingual, the ones whole in more than one tongue and at home in the space between.
  • …is drawn to myth as the truest map we have for what happens inside a life, and to the body as the place that knows before language arrives.
  • …believes deeply that we do not learn to relate to ourselves first and then to others — that self and other grow together, in relation, all the way through; and that connection is not the absence of rupture but the literacy to reconnect after it.
  • …is building a world where relationship itself is the curriculum, where a woman's descent is not a story of damage to be healed but a passage she can lead herself back from, and where depth and abundance belong in the same sentence.

And I attract women who share this — who have been to their own underworlds, who are done being led, and who are ready to return on their own terms.

Women in the second half of life who carry more than one culture in the body and have been pulled into an underworld they did not choose. They are literate, travelled, often the ones others lean on. They are done performing recovery. They are not looking for a coach to fix them; they are looking for a threshold, a myth large enough to hold what happened to them, and the company of women who will witness them being born into what comes next.

What Becomes Possible

This is not about recovering from what happened to you.

This is about discovering that you are fertile beyond childbirth — that you can conceive, in the dark, a work and a self you have not been yet, carry it the nine weeks it takes, and deliver it in your own time and your own place.

It is about the moment you stop waiting to be led and feel your own feet on the path up. About being witnessed — not fixed, witnessed — by women who descended beside you. About returning to your own languages, your own body, your own hour.

Fervent, where you were flat. Fertile, where you thought you were finished. Free.

Who This Is For — and Who It Is Not For

This is for you if

  • You have been to an underworld you did not choose, and you are done calling survival a personality.
  • You live and love across more than one culture or language, and you have never found a room that holds all of you.
  • You sense a life trying to be born through you — a work, a self, a next chapter — and you cannot translate it alone.
  • You are ready to lead yourself back, and to be witnessed doing it.

This is not for you if

  • You want to be rescued, fixed, or handed a healing arc with a tidy ending.
  • You want a fast method or a template, rather than a descent and a gestation that take the time they take.
  • You would rather stay underground, managing, than be seen returning.
The After-World

You move through your languages and your countries as one whole woman rather than several managed halves. What happened to you is behind you — not erased, integrated — the way ground holds the roots of what grows from it. You are fervent where you were flat. Fertile where you thought you were finished. Free.
